## Design Context

### Users
The primary audience is luxury wedding clients evaluating Evenaatii for premium planning and design support. They are typically browsing in a calm, aspirational mindset and want to feel emotional trust, aesthetic confidence, and execution certainty before they inquire.

Their main job is to decide whether Evenaatii can create a wedding experience that feels elevated, effortless, and personally meaningful. The interface should help them move from inspiration to confidence, then into a qualified inquiry.

### Brand Personality
Evenaatii should feel celebratory, emotional, and premium.

The tone should feel composed, intimate, refined, and high-touch rather than flashy or sales-heavy. The brand should communicate care, taste, and control without drifting into glossy luxury clichés.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction should be light-mode and editorial-luxury, with cinematic atmosphere balanced by restraint. It should feel like a private wedding journal or a premium printed keepsake rather than a loud event agency landing page.

References implied by the brief:
- cinematic full-bleed media
- quiet premium typography
- controlled motion that reveals structure gradually
- emotionally resonant imagery with elegant spatial composition

Anti-direction:
- glossy luxury
- loud colors
- overdecorated wedding-vendor templates
- shiny, overproduced marketing tropes

### Design Principles
1. Lead with atmosphere, then reveal structure.
2. Keep the luxury signal quiet and composed, never glossy or loud.
3. Make every section feel curated, not stacked.
4. Use motion as a reveal mechanism, not decoration.
5. Preserve emotional warmth while keeping conversion paths decisive.
